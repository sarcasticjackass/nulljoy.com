Electric Purgatory

Sometimes I try. This is what that looks like.

Every so often, I hit the bottom and whisper, “What if?”... and if the meds are working (or maybe failing spectacularly), I start seeing in color again. Not hope. Don’t get it twisted. But something that resembles meaning if you squint hard enough and keep the TV on static.

Electric Purgatory is where my better hallucinations live. A liminal loop of neon and noise, stitched together from half-formed absolutions and dangerously pretty lies. The sacred’s been hotwired. The divine’s having a panic attack in a dressing room made of mirrors and wires.

Here, beauty grins through cracked paint. Icons glitch. Skies burn. Televangelist robots scream repentance over a synth-wave choir while melancholy donkeys brood under acid rain. You know. Tuesday.

This is the gallery you come to when you've seen too much, and your brain needs a chemical rinse. Not to save yourself... but to make the damnation look cooler on the way down.
