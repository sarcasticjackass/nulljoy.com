Nightmare Log

I don’t dream anymore; only unravel.
Sleep, for me, is no longer a refuge but a trapdoor. What visits me there isn't whimsy or insight, but ruin. In this advanced state of psychological erosion, all ventures into the unconscious come bearing teeth. I’ve been haunted by the same few nightmares since I was eight—faithful companions in my nightly descent. I’ll document them here, alongside any new horrors that decide to crawl out and introduce themselves. Light sleepers have it easy. We get to wake up before the monsters finish their work.
