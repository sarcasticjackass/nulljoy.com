This document will self-destruct the instant I start believing in it.

I don’t know what’s true anymore.
But I’ve been alive long enough to collect fragments.
These are mine. Truths, lies, failures, and fears; auto-sorted by damage type.
Some of them broke me. Some of them saved me. Some are still on trial. 

Things I Believe (For Now):

  • Meaning is built, not found. And half the time it’s duct-taped together with nostalgia and caffeine.
  • Pain is a better teacher than comfort. Unfortunately.
  • Most people are just trying to survive the day without making eye contact with their own soul.
  • The universe doesn’t care. So I do.
  • Empathy isn’t weakness. It’s rebellion.
  • Creation doesn’t need a point. It just needs a pulse.

Stuff That Failed Me:

  • Hustle culture.
  • Blind faith.
  • “If you just stay positive, things will get better.”
  • Alarm clocks.
  • Obsessing over legacy instead of living.
  • Trying to live up to a version of myself that never really existed.
  • Supplements. The industry, the cultish pseudoscience, the influencers with shaker bottles and empty eyes. It's a multi-billion-dollar enema dressed as wellness. None of it ever helped me. Unless you count the placebo buzz right before the crash.
  • The myth of a “balanced life.” Nobody’s balanced. They’re just better at surfing chaos without face-planting. The keg is rolling. The tilt-a-whirl is spinning. I only stay upright by leaning into the extremes, not pretending the ride’s ever going to smooth out.
  • The idea that forgiveness can be taught. You either came into this world with that switch installed, or you didn’t. I didn’t. And no podcast, therapist, or inspirational quote has rewired that part of me yet.

Beautiful Lies I Keep Anyway:

  • That art can change the world.
  • That one more good day might tilt the scale.
  • That I’ll be remembered for the right things.
  • That redemption is just one act away.
  • That there’s still time to become who I’m supposed to be.
  • That something beautiful might still come from all this mess.

What I Hope Isn’t True:

  • That I peaked emotionally in a mall parking lot in 2002.
  • That some people are born assholes, without remedy.
  • That none of this meant anything.
  • That I’m the villain in someone else’s origin story.
  • That I’m just rearranging chairs on the deck of a ship already sunk.
  • That my silence did more damage than my words ever could.
  • That I’ll be remembered more for what I didn’t do than what I did; a legacy of hesitation, filed under “almost.”
  • That some people never change… and I am one of them.
