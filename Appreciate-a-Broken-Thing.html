How to Appreciate a Broken Thing

Not a sermon. Just a mirror with the cracks left in.

Some things don’t get fixed.
Some people don’t bounce back.
Sometimes the story doesn’t resolve... it just ends, mid-sentence, while the lights flicker.
And yet: there’s still beauty. Not in spite of the damage, but because of it.
Because it stood. Because it tried. Because it was, when it could’ve vanished.

This isn’t a guide to healing.
It’s a field manual for seeing clearly... the kind of clarity that hurts a little.
Welcome to the appreciation of what’s flawed, fallen, or just too strange to market. 

How to Appreciate a Broken Thing

(Or: A half-truth survival guide.​)

 1. Stop flinching
If it’s cracked, let it be cracked. Admiration doesn’t require repair. Look closer. Half the story lives in the fracture. 

 2. Beauty doesn’t beg.
It doesn’t raise its hand or wait its turn. Sometimes it’s hiding in a rust stain shaped like a heart, or a crying child with perfect pitch.

3. Scars are timestamps.
Not everything that’s damaged is doomed. Not everything whole is honest. Some things wear their survival like jewelry. Learn to nod at that.

4. Look away from the spotlight.
The best things live in the corner. Behind the curtain. Half-covered in dust. Fame is not a synonym for worth.

5. Ugly is just honest without a filter.
Try not to confuse prettiness with peace. Some of the loudest pain comes dressed in pearls and pastel. Some healing screams like hell and looks like junk.

6. If it makes you feel something, it works.
Art. People. You.
Even if it’s glitchy.
Even if it’s awkward.
Even if it’s limping across the finish line in a gas mask and glitter paint.

7. Not everything needs a reason.
Sometimes you love a thing because it echoes something inside you that words can’t pin down. That’s enough. That’s everything. 

8. Broken doesn’t mean useless.
A shattered clock still holds time... not in hours, but in memory. A torn book still knows how the story ends.
Stop measuring things by what they can still do for you. Some things deserve presence, not function.

9. Wholeness is overrated.
Everyone’s chasing some imagined version of “fixed,” like it's a finish line. It’s not.
Most of us are stitched together with compromise, old dreams, and duct tape. That’s not failure. That’s architecture.

10. Try doesn’t mean succeed.
Trying is sacred. It’s the rebellion of motion in the face of futility.
Even if the attempt collapses, even if the audience walks out, even if it all ends in static... the trying still matters.
The swing is beautiful, even when it misses.

11. Let the thing be what it is.
Stop sanding off the edges. Stop repainting what you don’t understand.
Sometimes the most respectful thing you can do is witness it honestly... in its current state, not its potential. 

12. Some messes aren’t yours to clean.
You can sit with the wreckage without sweeping it up.
Appreciation doesn’t mean ownership. It means presence… knowing how to stand in the room without needing to rearrange the furniture.

13. The ache is part of it.
If it didn’t sting, it wouldn’t hum like this.
The beauty lives because the damage rings out... like a bell cracked dead center, still singing every time it’s touched.
Don’t numb the resonance. Listen to it. 

14. It doesn’t have to be pretty to be beautiful.
Pretty is cosmetic. Beautiful is atmospheric.
Pretty fades in harsh light. Beautiful stays strange, even when the camera zooms out.
If you can’t explain why you’re drawn to it, you’re probably on the right track.

15. You are a broken thing too.
You know that already. But here’s the secret: that’s never been the dis-qualifier you think it is.
You’re chipped. Bruised. Strange in the most specific way.
And still... someone out there would stand in a gallery just to look at you longer.
